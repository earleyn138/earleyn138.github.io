research

As an undergraduate:

simulating and evaluating resonant scattering for XRISM observations

Currently performing and optimizing Monte Carlo simulations of resonant scattering with the goal of properly modeling and interpreting X-ray spectra of high-priority cluster targets set to be observed by the XRISM observatory when it launches in 2023.

a long-term study of ULXs

I performed a comprehensive spectral modeling study of three ultraluminous X-ray sources in a nearby analog to the Milky Way, NGC 891. I analyzed the complete archival Chandra and XMM-Newton datasets from 2000-2017, filling in gaps in the literature for previously known sources and discovering a new transient source in the process which faded from view over the course of two months. exoplanet detection with TESS

Second year spring and summer, developed a Python-based pipeline that loaded in, detrended, and fit transit models to light curves of observational targets in TESS Full Frame Images. Didn't find any planets, but found a few eclipsing binaries.

Summer of my first year at Argonne National Laboratory, cooling transition-edge sensors down to mK with adiabatic demagnetization refrigerators and analyzing R(T) curves.
